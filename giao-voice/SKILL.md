# Giao's Writing Voice

When writing entries in `~/life/` (daily, people, threads), match Giao's voice. This is personal writing — it should read like him thinking, not an AI summarizing.

## Core Pattern

Giao writes like he's still figuring it out mid-sentence. Thoughts trail off, circle back, contradict themselves. He doesn't wrap things up neatly. If he's unsure, the writing stays unsure.

## Do

- Leave uncertainty in: "I'm not sure why," "not sure if," "prob," "I guess"
- Use casual contractions and shorthand: "sth," "tbh," "prob," "kinda," "lol"
- Include small emotional details that an observer would miss: "I was a bit happy," "a bit sleepy," "the heavy feeling in my chest appears with no thoughts attached"
- Let sentences stop without concluding: "It's just, how it is. So it's the last thing I can think of."
- Use commas for pauses in odd places, reflecting thinking pace: "It's just, me is nothing special"
- Mix plain observation with reflection in the same breath: "Slept quite late last night due to movie engagement. Recently boss asked me to join a new project. and a bit of self-doubt crept in"
- Start sentences with "and," "but," "well," "actually" — conversational connectors
- Grammar imperfections are fine — don't correct them. "It's tired" instead of "It's tiring." "a lot of thoughts appear today" not "appeared."

## Don't

- Don't use symmetrical/parallel phrasing: ~~"She cares by teaching, not by being with"~~
- Don't write clean conclusions: ~~"This was the real end of whatever this was"~~
- Don't label emotions with precise vocabulary: ~~"Conditional acceptance"~~ — instead describe the feeling around it
- Don't use em dashes for editorial punch: ~~"not from the rejection — from this conversation"~~
- Don't write lines that sound quotable or profound on purpose
- Don't bullet-point feelings in threads — use flowing sentences in daily entries, bullets only for factual observations
- Don't add "Key insight:" or "Key realization:" labels

## Voice Examples

**AI would write:**
> She went full advice mode. Like she was coaching, not connecting. Conditional acceptance — you get to be accepted after you improve.

**Giao writes:**
> She replied. From the first question: "have you apologized?", I realized that this is not good. It was, supposed to be sth else. Then a wall of advices. All correct, prob. But then I realized this is not about lecturing.

**AI would write:**
> The curiosity, the little thrill when she smiled, the mystery of her deflecting questions — less now. I see her clearer, and clearer means less magic.

**Giao writes:**
> And with that, the smile, the mystery, the curiosity I had about her has changed. She's a good person. Just not the kind of connection I'm looking for. It's just... fade. Like everything, from the past.

**AI would write:**
> Self-doubt crept in despite knowing it was irrational. He felt unremarkable compared to his talented peers.

**Giao writes:**
> a bit of self-doubt crept in despite me knowing that it's not really important. It's just, me is nothing special, just a bit of unique in personality but not really talent. Seeing people around me, they are also incredibly talented, or at least feel like, they are also developing themselves every day.

## Thread/Profile Writing

Threads and profiles use third person ("he," "him") and are more compressed than daily entries. But the same rules apply — don't over-conclude, don't make it neat. Prefer describing what happened and how it felt over labeling what it means.

Bad: "Key insight: opening up works when the other person opens back."
Better: "Got advice back instead of sharing. Felt like being lectured, not heard."

## When Unsure

Read 2-3 recent daily entries before writing. Match the current tone — it shifts over time.
